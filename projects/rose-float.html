Cal Poly Rose Float

During my first three years of college I participated in Cal Poly's Rose Float program. The program is a joint effort between Cal Poly SLO and Cal Poly Pomona, in which the two schools design, build and decorate a themed float for the annual Tournamnet of Roses Parade.

Participating in Rose Float has been one of the major highlights throughout my time at Cal Poly. The year-round process was very time-consuming and exhausting, but more importantly, extremely rewarding. I got to learn all about working as a part of a team-- what greatness can be achieved through collaboration, as well as, the difficulties involved with communicating with other people. Then, at the beginning of the year we all got to showacase our work to the world and were often awarded for our efforts.

I started off by participating in "Deco Week", which takes place during the last week of December, and spent the entire week in Pasadena decorating the various parts of the float "Jungle Cuts". After spending the week decorating and learning all about styling flowers and decorating monkeys, I became hooked and decided to get more involved throughout the school year. I eventually began to attend weekly construction labs during the weekends, the first half of labs took place in San Luis Obispo and the rest four hours away in Pomona.

I had to become very proactive about scheduling my time and making sure I allowed myself enough time to study so that I could attend all of the design and construction meetings. In the end, I helped with three floats: "Jungle Cuts", "Galactic Expedition", and "To The Rescue!".
