Transamerica Internship

Over the past three summers I have been grateful to have taken part in an internship at Transamerica in Los Angeles. During my time there I worked as a software developer working on internal web applications for the companies' retirement solutions branch.

I remember feeling very nervous the first time I showed up at the office as an IT intern in 2012. It was the first time really experiencing what it was like to be a software developer and I am happy that year-after-year I was asked to return. I definitely felt that I was contributing to a large project and happy that some of my work will be used by employees at the company to facilitate the way they conduct business.

I spent many hours becoming acquainted with tools and languages I initially knew nothing about, but that I am now very familiar with. I mostly conducted front-end development using Flex and Actionscript, but I also worked a bit with Java in the back-end. I learned the great benefits (and nuisances) of using an IDE like Eclipse, as well as, SVN for version control. My experiences at Transamerica have initiated me to pursue greater knowledge in front-end software development-- prompting me to learn more about other tools and languages, such as, HTML, Javascript, CSS, and PHP.
